Certifications, Explained Simply

What all the terms mean.

SDVOSB stands for Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned Small Business. It means a veteran with a service-connected disability owns most of the company and actually runs it, not just on paper. Pioneer Federal Group qualifies because Desi, a 100 percent disabled veteran, owns 51 percent and makes the final calls. You can read the government's official rules on the Small Business Administration's website.

We already meet the ownership and control rules. The formal certification paperwork with the Small Business Administration is in progress.

A NAICS code is just a number the government uses to sort businesses by what kind of work they do. It helps agencies match a contract to the right company. Pioneer Federal Group holds codes for facilities support, construction, freight and hazmat transport, grounds maintenance, moving services, and general support. You can look up any code yourself on the U.S. Census Bureau's website.

SAM.gov is the government's official system for registering as a contractor. A company has to be listed there before it can bid on or win a federal contract. Our registration, along with our UEI and CAGE code, is currently in progress. You can visit SAM.gov directly to see how it works.
